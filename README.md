This project showcases an interactive Super Store sales and profit dashboard built in Power BI to analyze performance across regions, categories, and time. It is designed for business users to quickly understand trends, key drivers, and improvement opportunities.

1. Project Title and Headline
Title: Super Store Sales Performance Dashboard

Headline: Interactive Power BI dashboard to track sales, profit, and customer segments across multiple dimensions for data‑driven decision making.

2. Short Description and Purpose
This project analyzes Super Store sales and profit data to provide a single, visual view of business performance across regions, product categories, segments, and shipping modes.

The purpose is to help managers monitor KPIs, identify high‑value segments, and understand where revenue and profit are growing or declining over time.

3. Tech Stack
Power BI Desktop for data modeling, DAX measures, and dashboard design.

Power Query for data cleaning, transformation, and calculated columns.

Microsoft Excel / CSV as the original data file format.

Power BI Visualization layer for charts, maps, and interactive slicers/filters.

4. Data Source
Super Store transactional sales dataset containing order‑level details.

Key fields include Order Date, Ship Mode, Customer Segment, Region, State, Product Category/Sub‑Category, Quantity, Sales, and Profit.

5. Features and Highlights
5.1 Business Problem
Management needs a clear view of “where and how” the store is making money across different states, customer segments, and product categories.

The goal is to quickly identify profitable areas, loss‑making segments, and trends over time without manually going through raw spreadsheets.

5.2 Dashboard Goal
Provide a single interactive dashboard to monitor total Sales, Profit, Quantity, and average delivery time.

Enable drill‑down by Region, State, Category, Segment, Payment Mode, Ship Mode, and Sub‑Category to support faster and more informed business decisions.

5.3 Key Visuals and Why They Are Used
KPI cards for Quantity, Sales, Profit, and Average Delivery Date to give an instant snapshot of overall performance.

Map visual (Sum of Sales and Profit by State) to show geographic performance and quickly highlight high‑ and low‑performing states.

Line charts for Monthly Sales and Monthly Profit (by Year) to track seasonality and year‑over‑year trends.

Bar charts for Sales by Ship Mode, Category, and Sub‑Category to compare contributions of different shipping types and product lines.

Donut/pie charts for Sum of Sales by Payment Mode and Segment to understand customer behavior and the share of each segment or payment method.

5.4 Business Impact and Insights
Helps identify which regions and states generate the most revenue and profit, supporting region‑wise strategy and resource allocation.

Reveals top‑performing and under‑performing product categories and sub‑categories, guiding inventory planning, promotions, and product focus.

Shows how shipping modes and customer segments affect sales and profitability, enabling optimization of delivery options and targeted marketing.

Overall, the dashboard converts raw sales data into clear, actionable insights that support data‑driven decision making for the Super Store business.
